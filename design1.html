HIKE CHALLENGE

HikeChallenge is an app that guides users through a vibrant journey to collect memories from Switzerland hiking adventures everywhere they go.

BACKGROUND

HikeChallenge came to life one sunny Thursday afternoon in Zurich, Switzerland, during an extended lunch session. We came up with the idea to design a digital product to help foreigners interact with nature in Switzerland.

Our target audience includes adventurous explorers, nature enthusiasts, and newcomers to Switzerland.

We aim to enhance the hiking experience for foreign nature lovers of all ages, prioritizing personal enjoyment and unique experiences.

Affinity Diagram

To analyze user research, we used an affinity diagram. These notes reveal behavioral patterns from interviews, helping us understand user goals and preferences, guiding our design to align with their background and expectations.

Notable Quotes by Interviewees

Q: What does “connecting with nature” mean to you?

“Not looking at the computer or screen all the time, being forced to be away from your thoughts, and hanging out with my friends while also exercising.”

Q: With whom do you usually share your time in nature (e.g., alone, with friends, or with family)?

“Alone, it's hard to find friends with the same energy or who want to do the same things in nature.”

FOUR DESIGN DIRECTIONS

Our Affinity Diagram pinpointed the areas where people struggle the most in their interactions with Swiss nature - the aspects we should be working on. Now, it's time to turn these insights into actionable design directions.

  • 1. Having a clear hierarchy allows users to identify what they are searching for
  • 2. Incentivizing users to get in touch with nature
  • 3. Making the process of finding like-minded friends easier
  • 4. Making the interaction with other users on the platform more natural

INITIAL IDEAS

After completing the Afinity Diagram and defining our Four Design Directions, we came up with some initial solutions.

The core idea was this: Fuse various outdoor activities with hiking, resulting in what we termed 'Challenges.'

These Challenges represent unique combinations. For instance, consider the 'Yoga Pose Challenge,' comprising a sequence of three yoga poses. Participants would commit to undertaking the Challenge and, within an agreed timeframe, execute the designated poses at specific locations. Successful completion of the Challenge would earn them the coveted Challenge Stars.

MVP ELEMENTS

We designed wireframes for screens featuring essential functionalities to launch and operationalize a Minimum Viable Product (MVP).
  • Homepage Featuring User Journey Map: Displays users' personal Switzerland hiking adventures
  • Challenges Page: For users to initiate and accomplish challenges
  • Swipe Page: Enabling users to discover like-minded individuals
  • Extra elements: Profile, and Group Learderboard

USER RESEARCH TESTING

After refining our prototype, we conducted user research testing to gain insights into the friction points of the three task flows:

  • 1. Browse & post hiking challenges to friends
  • 2. Check notifications and group leaderboard
  • 3. Swipe through profiles & send friend requests to like-minded

User Testing Insights

  • 1. Enhancing Challenge Variety to Match User Interests

    • Our users expressed a desire for a wider range of challenge types to cater to diverse interests.

  • 2. Shifting Focus of Swipe-to-Match Functionality

    • We shift the focus from appearance-based swiping to align with users' completed challenges and interests.

FINALIZING OUR INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

Based on the analysis of our usability test, I proceeded to develop the final information architecture, outlining our product's key functions, information flow, and arrangement.

HIGH-FIDELITY PROTOTYPES

I then proceeded to Figma to create hi-fi prototypes based on our information architecture. Play with the figma prototype here. Below are some key interactions of our product:

Post a new challnege to friends

Users can quickly share predefined challenges with selected friends, set a time frame, and inspire their participation.

Create a new challenge

Once users become acquainted with the app, they can craft their own challenges, customizing the name, description, rules, and rewards to align with their preferences.

Check group chat and leaderboard

Users can access the group chat for communication and check the group leaderboard to track standings and boost motivation through friendly competition.

Swipte or Scan to add new friends

With a swipe gesture or scanning a QR code, users can easily expand their network and invite new friends, simplifying the connection process and fostering community.
